About Me

I graduated from St. John's University in Minnesota in 2018 with a major in computer science and a minor in mathematics. In the fall of 2018, I began my Ph.D. in computer science at the University of Oregon, advised by Dr. Dejing Dou and Dr. Daniel Lowd. I am now in the third year of the Ph.D. program, and I am researching techniques to make deep neural networks more interpretable while helping to TA CIS 210. My hope is to leverage the knowledge I gain to help build provably safe and beneficial deep learning systems.

In my free time I love to read and play basketball, guitar, and chess.
